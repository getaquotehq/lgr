Why this exists

Every lead you buy, someone else is pocketing the spread.

A reseller buys traffic and sells you the result at a fixed price. When their ad costs drop, that gap is theirs - you never see a cent of it. When their costs rise, your price follows or your lead gets shared harder. You carry the market either way and you never own the upside. Pay-per-lead is a toll, not a partnership.

FAQ

The questions installers actually ask.

Why isn't it a fixed cost per lead?

Because a fixed cost per lead only ever protects us. Ad platforms don't charge a flat rate - what it costs to reach a homeowner swings with the season, the auction and the rebate cycle. A per-lead reseller pockets the difference when costs fall and quietly shares your lead harder when they rise. Either way you carry the market and never see the upside.

So we do it the other way around. You pay one flat fee for the cycle and we run a dynamic cost per lead underneath it. When ad platform CPMs spike, your guaranteed floor still lands - we absorb the extra cost, not you. When conditions are good and costs drop, that same fee buys more leads and the volume climbs. You're insulated on the bad months and you share the good ones. The price is fixed; the value moves in your favour.

What does "exclusive" actually mean here?

It means every lead goes to one installer - you - and nobody else. Ever. Not sold to three of your competitors, not resold a week later as an "aged" lead, not shared with a marketplace.

And we don't ask you to take that on trust. Every homeowner consents to be contacted by your business, by name, at the moment they submit the form. The exclusivity is built into the capture, not promised afterwards - there's structurally nowhere else the lead can go.

How are leads delivered?

However suits how you work. Leads can land as an SMS and email to your phone the moment they come in, and we can push them straight into your CRM if you run one. Most installers take all three so nothing slips.

On top of that, our AI texts the homeowner in your name within about 60 seconds of them enquiring - so by the time the lead reaches you, the conversation's already warm.

Is there any guarantee I'll get leads?

Yes. Every asset carries a guaranteed floor - a minimum number of leads for the 30-day cycle, committed in writing. If we fall short of it, you don't chase us: you either get the campaign kept running at our cost until the floor lands, or a pro-rata refund on the shortfall at the floor rate. Your call.

What we don't dress up: the floor is the guarantee, and the typical range above it is exactly that - typical, not promised. We'd rather set a floor we'll always hit than a number we'd have to weasel out of. What we can't guarantee is that a given homeowner answers or signs - no honest lead company can - which is why speed-to-contact and exclusivity matter as much as volume.

Am I locked into a contract?

No. It's month to month, billed in advance. Cancel any time before your next cycle starts - no lock-in, no exit fee, no notice period. If it's not working for you, you leave. The floor and the exclusivity do the work of keeping you, not a contract.
